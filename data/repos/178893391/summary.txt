LeftWM is a tiling window manager, written in Rust, designed for Linux environments, providing users with an alternative to traditional desktop environments. Its core function is to manage application windows, arranging them in a tiled layout, promoting efficiency and customizability. Key features include extensive theming support, configurable keybindings, workspace management, and EWMH compliance. Users can customize their experience through themes, configured using the `RON` format, and manage settings like keybindings, layouts, and workspace arrangements. As a standalone application, it targets Linux users seeking a lightweight and customizable window management solution.
